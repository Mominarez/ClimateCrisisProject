Billionaire Emissions

The average billionaire emits 8,194 tons of carbon dioxide equivalent per year.

Disproportionate Emissions

Since the 1990s, the richest 1% have emitted more than double the carbon of the poorest half of the population.

Deaths from Carbon Emissions

The carbon emissions of the richest 1% in 2019 were enough to change global temperatures and lead to an estimated 1.3 million deaths. Emissions of the richest 1% will cause 1.3 million heat-related deaths between 2020 and 2030—roughly the equivalent of the entire population of Dallas.

Super-Rich vs. Humanity

In 2019, the super-rich 1% were responsible for more carbon emissions than 66% of humanity (5 billion people).

Corporate Responsibility

Corporations are a major contributor to climate change, with 100 companies responsible for 71% of global greenhouse gas emissions since 1988.

Future Temperature Rise

If fossil fuels continue to be extracted at the same rate as they were between 1988 and 2017, global temperatures could rise by 4°C by the end of the century.

Meat Production Impact

Meat production is responsible for 14.5% of global greenhouse gas emissions. This includes methane from cows and sheep, nitrous oxide from animal waste, and carbon dioxide released when forests are cut down to create grasslands for livestock.
